How to DIY your own soaps!

Melt & Pour Soap Bases

Melt and pour soaps are a new way of soap making without dealing with adding in dangerous lye. In less than an hour you will be able to make quality soap in a fun and quite inexpensive way, all in the comfort of your home. Try your hands on our melt and pour soap bases!
Grade: Cosmetic

Instructions:

• Melt soap base by double boiling it to ensure that the soap base does not become overheated or scorched. (Note: Melting point is between 50-60 Celcius)
• Stir the soap base gently to avoid formation of bubbles
• When fully melted, add in your own colorants, dried flowers, carrier oils, essential oils or cosmetic fragrances. (Note: Essential oils or cosmetic fragrances should be between 1% -2% of the total soap base weight)
• Pour the melted soap base mixture into the moulds before the soap base is hardened.
• Spritz rubbing alcohol on the soap to remove any bubbles on the surface.
• Wrap the soap with cling wrap if desired to keep out moisture and prevent the soap from “sweating”.
• Allow 6 hours for the soap to harden before use.

Ingredients:

White Soap Base: Coconut Oil, Palm Oil, Castor Oil, Stearic Acid, Glycerin, Sucrose, Sodium Hydroxide, Decyl Glucoside, Cocoamide KET, Propylene Glycol, Sorbitol, Citric Acid, Titanium Dioxide
Transparent Soap Base: Coconut Oil, Palm Oil, Castor Oil, Stearic Acid, Glycerin, Sucrose, Sodium Hydroxide, Decyl Glucoside, Cocoamide KET, Propylene Glycol, Sorbitol, Citric Acid
Caution: This is a cosmetic raw material and is meant for external use only in cosmetic formulations. As with all of our materials, it should not be taken internally.
